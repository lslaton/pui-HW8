Adidashboard

Designing a data dashboard to help stakeholders incorporate global user data and experience.

Context: Interaction Design Studio 1, CMU
Team: Shai Bhardwaj, Eric Li, Zach Van Cleef, Janelle Wen
Timeline: October-November, 2020

Prompt

In our Interaction Design Studio 1 class, our task was to design a dashboard that displays a set of data to the user that provides context and alerts the viewer to important events as they occur. We were given two resources for this project: three personas of employees from Adidas- an executive, a product manager, and a designer; and Amazon review data for their flagship shoe and biggest competitor. And with that, we were off!

"How might we incorporate the needs of various personas to create a data visualization that incorporates user experience?"
Final Design

Adidashboard

We had a blast going through the process. It was a challenge to go through the data and truly understand the personas, while not compromising the needs of the user. This was critical to keep the intent in our minds throughout the design process. A great lesson that we learned was to not lean on the aesthetic choices in explaining data. Although it is visually appealing to display information one way, it may be an ineffective way to understand it. The dashboard is meant to be viewed quickly so that the team can understand trends and take action. We will take this into consideration for future dashboard designs.

My Role

In the early stages of the process, the team worked collaboratively to evaluate the data and personas and to generate insights to be used in our dashboard. Each team member was additionally responsible for a section of the dashboard, which included generating iterations, prototyping, animating motion, as well as delivering that portion of the pitch. I worked specifically on the competitor sales section, as well as editing our team's pitch to maintain flow and communicate the value to stakeholders. Additionally, I worked with my team to log our progress in our process book including collecting screenshots and writing the content.

Design Process

Models
In the current state model, the stakeholders make their actions based on their own interests and goals without paying full attention to the customer needs, and therefore leading to problems and negative feedback from customers. We decided to display this horizontally to show the one-way direction of stakeholder concerns. In the future state model, we flipped the horizontal diagram to be vertical so that top-down user needs can be addressed, with the Dashboard inserted between customer needs and stakeholder goals to serve as the connecting and driving force between the two. Based on the critiques we received, the next steps of our project was to find the exact commonalities between customers and stakeholders and figure out precisely the data we need to bridge the gap between those two.

Iteration 1 & 2
Initially, our team struggled to hone in on the specific needs of each persona and how they might come together to create a dashboard that could cater to each of them. Our first iteration of the dashboard had five main sections: Global Sales Data, Web Analytics, Customer Reviews, Product Traction, and Competitor Analysis. It was a start, but didn't cohesively come together to create deeper insights or value to our personas.

After receiving our critiques, I began to think critically about our insights thus far and how we might leverage what we know into a cohesive vision. I realized that the personas all had some interest in the user experience in various forms, and was able to propose this idea to the team to use in our third iteration of the dashboard.

Iteration 3 & 4
Our third and fourth iterations of the dashboard incorporated motion to draw the viewer's eye to important events as they unfold. We originally had every piece of the dashboard moving, but quickly realized that it was disjointed and overwhelming. We decided to incorporate our personas' global mindset and create different modes for the dashboard that showed data based on geographic region: worldwide, Europe, and North America. The Competitor Sales, Web Analytics, and Reviews sections would move to update with each region's data to learn more about trends and specific user experiences. The modes could be changed with a physical control of a map where the user could press specific regions to change the view.

Pitch
For our pitch script, our main goal was to put customers first. To emphasize this, we showed how the stakeholders could use each part of our dashboard to make a better experience for the customers. During our first iteration, our presentation’s visual was one slide that displayed the whole dashboard, and each person explained a certain widget. The main two critiques we received were that we talked too fast and that the audience struggled to find which widget each person was talking about because it felt out of order. To address these critiques, we parsed our script and cut down on excessive explanations, and added small examples to connect the stakeholders to the board. We additionally rethought the order to correspond to the flow of the dashboard, and made individual slides that enlarged the particular widget to draw the viewer’s eye as the respective presenter spoke about it.
